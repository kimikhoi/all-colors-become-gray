# Artist Statement

“All Colors Become Gray” is a generative digital artwork composed from the coexistence of all possible RGB color combinations.

Each pixel represents an individual and irreducible color identity. Yet when all colors are presented simultaneously at sufficient density, the human eye no longer distinguishes individuality and instead perceives an almost uniform gray field.

The work explores a paradox of diversity: total inclusion may visually converge into indistinction.

Rather than rejecting diversity itself, the artwork raises philosophical questions about perception, consensus, averaging, entropy, and the limits of human cognition in highly heterogeneous systems.

The circular composition symbolizes a closed perceptual universe in which objective diversity exists physically, while subjective uniformity emerges perceptually.

This artwork was generated algorithmically and published as a conceptual exploration of perception and collective identity.
